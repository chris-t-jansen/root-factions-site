## Overview

As the Eyrie Dynasties, your <term>Decree</term> is the heart of your faction, both as its biggest strength, and its biggest weakness. The Decree has four columns, ordered from left to right: **Recruit**, **Move**, **Battle**, and **Build**. At the start of each turn, you're required to place one or two cards into the Decree, putting each into the column of your choice. Each card placed into the Decree represents an action you must then take on every turn thereafter, in a clearing matching the suit of the card. The columns of the Decree must be resolved in order from left to right, but the cards within each column can be fulfilled in any order. For example, if you had two mouse-suited cards and one bird-suited card in your Battle column, you'd have to battle twice in mouse-suited clearings (the same clearing or different clearings) and once in a clearing of your choice (since bird cards are wild), but you could choose to do those battles in any order.

The Recruit and Build actions of the Decree both relate to the Eyrie Dynasties's single building type, <term>roosts</term>. These roosts serve as bases from which you'll recruit more warriors, and there can only be one roost in each clearing on the map. Roosts are also how the Eyrie Dynasties earn victory points, scoring points at the end of every turn based on the number of roosts you have on the map. Just be wary, however: you only have seven roosts total, and if you build them all, you won't be able to build more on future turns unless some are destroyed.

Rallying and unifying the Eyrie Dynasties is the <term>leader</term> card. The leader provides you with a special ability while in power to help you reclaim the Woodland, and also comes with two <term>loyal visiers</term>, faction-unique bird cards placed into columns designated by the leader card.

If you find yourself unable to complete any one of the actions dictated by the cards in your Decree, you immediately <term>turmoil</term>. When this happens, you'll lose a victory point for every bird-suited card in your decree, including your loyal visiers. Then, you must discard all the cards in your Decree (minus the loyal visier cards) and remove your current leader card. Then, pick a new leader that has not yet suffered turmoil to replace the previous leader, and move the loyal visiers to the Decree columns dictated by the new leader. Finally, you'll cease resolving your Decree on that turn, losing the opportunity to take any actions beyond the one that caused you to turmoil.

## Strategy

The Eyrie Dynasties have a relatively straightforward mechanism for earning victory points, scoring simply for having roosts on the map. As such, Eyrie Dynasties strategy is largely focused on how you organize the Decree so that you have enough actions to defend yourself and interfere with your opponents, while not always being on the verge of turmoil.

### The Opening

Of the four leaders available to the Eyrie Dynasties, the Charismatic and Despot leaders are considered by far the best, with the other two typically only being used under special circumstances. The Charismatic leader lends itself towards a more aggressive playstyle, with its double-recruiting ability allowing you to quickly get many warriors onto the board, but requiring you to intentionally lose warriors in battle enough so that you don't turmoil on the Recruit column of your next turn. The Despot leader, in contrast, leans towards a slower, more methodical approach, slowly spreading and building roosts while using its special ability to score extra points from battling opponent's buildings and tokens, but wary of losing too many warriors due to its comparatively lower ability to replace them. Both playstyles have pros and cons, so pick the one that appeals to you; if at some point in the game you turmoil, it's probably a reasonable time to try the other approach.
