## Overview

As the Eyrie Dynasties, your <term>Decree</term> is the heart of your faction, both as its biggest strength, and its biggest weakness. The Decree has four columns, ordered from left to right: **Recruit**, **Move**, **Battle**, and **Build**. At the start of each turn, you're required to place one or two cards into the Decree, putting each into the column of your choice without limit. Each card placed into the Decree represents an action you must then take on every turn thereafter, in a clearing matching the suit of the card. The columns of the Decree must be resolved in order from left to right, but the cards within each column can be fulfilled in any order. For example, if you had two mouse-suited cards and one bird-suited card in your Battle column, you'd have to battle twice in mouse-suited clearings (the same clearing or different clearings) and once in a clearing of your choice (since bird cards are wild), but you could choose to do those battles in any order.

The Recruit and Build actions of the Decree both relate to the Eyrie Dynasties's single building type, <term>roosts</term>. These roosts serve as bases from which you'll recruit more warriors, and there can only be one roost in each clearing on the map. Roosts are also how the Eyrie Dynasties earn victory points, scoring points at the end of every turn based on the number of roosts you have on the map. Just be wary, however: you only have seven roosts total, and if you build them all, you won't be able to build more on future turns unless some are destroyed.

Rallying and unifying the Eyrie Dynasties is the <term>leader</term> card. The leader provides you with a special ability while in power to help you reclaim the Woodland, and also comes with two <term>loyal visiers</term>, bird cards placed into columns designated by the leader card.
