## Strategy

As a **Militant** faction that starts the game with an intimidating board presence (a warrior in every clearing using Advanced Setup), it can be tempting to believe that it's your job to police the board, using your substantial army to keep anyone from running away with the game. ***Do not fall into this trap.*** As the Marquise de Cat, your single biggest weakness is the number of actions you can take each turn: 3, plus 1 per bird card you spend. Now consider the demands your building-based scoring engine places on you: defend existing buildings to protect your economy, take new clearings for their empty building slots, and construct new buildings. To simply accomplish those every turn, you usually need to spend your 3 turn actions on Recruit, March, and Build. There's barely time for a single Battle action, much less the number needed to police the rest of the table. Your actions are your most limited resource, so hoard bird cards like a doomsday prepper and do not let the table talk you into working unpaid overtime.

If you've made it to the final third of the score track without being disintegrated by a screaming ball of eagles or made obsolete by a circus of badgers, congrats! You've made it further than many players do with the Marquise de Cat.
